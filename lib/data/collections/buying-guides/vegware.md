Vegware is the UK's leading brand of plant-based, compostable food packaging. Every product in the range is certified to EN 13432, the European standard for industrial compostability, which means it breaks down fully in commercial composting facilities. For food service businesses looking to move away from conventional plastics without compromising on performance, Vegware offers a single-brand solution that covers cups, containers, bags, wraps, and lids. This guide explains what the range includes, how to choose the right products, and what to consider before committing to bulk orders.

## Key Factors to Consider

### What Vegware Actually Is

Vegware is a brand, not a material. The range uses several different base materials depending on the product type: bagasse (sugarcane fibre) for rigid containers and clamshells, PLA (a plant-based polymer derived from cornstarch) for clear cups and lids, and sustainably sourced kraft paperboard with PLA linings for hot cups and bags. All of these materials are industrially compostable, but they behave differently in use. Bagasse handles heat and grease well. PLA is clear and rigid but warps above 40°C. Kraft cups with PLA lining perform much like conventional paper cups but break down in composting. Knowing which material sits behind each product helps you choose the right item for each menu application.

### The Product Range

The Vegware collection on Afida covers most of what a takeaway, cafe, or catering operation needs:

- **Bagasse containers:** Clamshells from 5in to 9in, burger boxes (standard and heavyweight), square lunch boxes, multi-compartment boxes, chip trays, and gourmet bases from 340ml up to 1800ml. These handle hot, greasy food without softening or leaking.
- **Lids:** Bagasse lids, PLA clear lids, and window lids in multiple sizes (Size 3, 4, and 5). Matching lids to containers by size number is essential. PLA lids give visibility for cold food displays; bagasse lids suit hot dishes and stacking.
- **Hot cups:** Single wall and double wall in 10oz, 12oz, 16oz, and 20oz. Available in kraft brown, white, printed designs (Green Britain, Green Tree, Gallery, Feel Good), and plain black. All 89-Series with standardised lid fit.
- **Cold cups:** PLA clear cups in the 96-Series (12oz, 16oz, 20oz) and paper cold cups in the 89-Series (10oz, 12oz, 16oz). PLA cups suit smoothies, juices, and iced drinks where visibility matters.
- **Cup lids:** White fibre hot cup lids (90mm for 89-Series). One lid size covers 12oz and 16oz cups in the same series.
- **Bags and wraps:** Kraft flat bags (white and brown, multiple sizes), therma bags for hot food, glassine bags with NatureFlex windows, kraft window bags, and ovenable wrap. Therma bags keep food warm during delivery; glassine bags work well for pastries and sandwiches.

### Sizing and Compatibility

Vegware uses a numbered sizing system for containers and lids. Gourmet bases and their lids are grouped by Size 3, Size 4, and Size 5. A Size 4 lid fits all Size 4 gourmet bases regardless of capacity. This simplifies stock management considerably: you can offer 340ml, 500ml, and 650ml portions using different bases but the same lid. Before ordering, confirm which size group each base falls into and stock the matching lids accordingly.

For cups, the 89-Series covers hot cups and paper cold cups with a consistent 89mm rim diameter. The 96-Series covers PLA cold cups with a 96mm rim. Do not mix lids between series.

### Sustainability and Certification

Every Vegware product carries EN 13432 certification for industrial composting. This is a genuine, audited standard, not a vague "eco-friendly" claim. However, industrial composting requires commercial facilities that process at sustained high temperatures. Not all UK council waste collections accept compostable packaging, and putting Vegware in a home compost bin will not produce the same results.

Before marketing your packaging as compostable to customers, check whether your local waste collection or a nearby commercial composter accepts these materials. If they do, Vegware gives you a credible, certified sustainability story. If they do not, the packaging will likely end up in landfill regardless of its certification. Being upfront about this builds more trust than overpromising.

### Cost and Value

Vegware products carry a premium over generic equivalents. Double wall hot cups start from around £7 per pack of 25 (roughly 28p per cup), compared to 15-20p for a non-branded compostable alternative. Bagasse clamshells range from £9 to £28 per case depending on size and weight. The premium buys you consistent quality, a recognised brand that customers associate with sustainability, and a single certification standard across your entire packaging setup.

The cost argument works best when you are already committed to compostable packaging. If you are comparing Vegware against standard plastic or polystyrene, the per-unit cost is significantly higher. But if the comparison is Vegware against other compostable brands, the price gap narrows and the consistency of the range becomes the advantage: one supplier, one certification, and products that are designed to work together.

### Heavyweight vs Standard Containers

Vegware offers heavyweight versions of several bagasse clamshells and burger boxes. Heavyweight options use thicker walls and reinforced hinges, making them better suited to saucy, heavy, or stacked dishes. Standard weight containers handle most dry or lightly sauced food well and cost less per unit. If your menu includes loaded burgers, curries, or dishes with gravy, test the heavyweight option before settling on standard. The hinge on a standard clamshell can weaken with very heavy contents or repeated opening and closing.

### Branding Considerations

Vegware products carry the Vegware branding and design language (Green Tree, Feel Good, Gallery prints, etc.). This is a selling point if your customers recognise and trust the brand, which many environmentally conscious consumers do. However, it means your packaging carries someone else's branding rather than your own. For businesses that want their own logo on compostable cups, custom printing is available on minimum orders (typically 5,000+ units). Alternatively, a branded sleeve or sticker over a plain Vegware cup gives you the best of both: certified compostable packaging with your own identity on it.

## Pro Tips

- Start by mapping your menu to Vegware's size groups. If most of your portions fit Size 4 containers, you only need one lid SKU for multiple base sizes, which cuts stock complexity and waste.
- Order sample packs of both standard and heavyweight clamshells and test them with your heaviest, sauciest dish. The difference in performance is noticeable and the heavyweight cost is justified if it prevents leaks and customer complaints.
- If you serve both hot and cold drinks, standardise on the 89-Series where possible. A single lid fitting your 12oz and 16oz hot cups reduces the number of products you need to manage behind the counter.
- Check your local council's food waste or commercial composting arrangements before printing "100% compostable" on your menus. The claim is technically accurate, but only if the infrastructure exists to process it.

## Summary

Vegware offers a comprehensive, certified compostable packaging range that covers hot food containers, cups, lids, bags, and wraps under a single brand. The main advantages are consistency across the range, EN 13432 certification on everything, and a brand name that carries weight with sustainability-minded customers. The trade-off is a per-unit premium over generic alternatives and limited scope for custom branding without minimum order quantities. For food service businesses committed to compostable packaging, stocking Vegware simplifies purchasing, ensures compliance, and gives your operation a credible environmental position that stands up to scrutiny.
