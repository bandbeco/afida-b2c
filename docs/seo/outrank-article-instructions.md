- What makes packaging "sustainable":
  - Made from renewable resources
  - Can be recycled, composted, or biodegraded
  - Minimal environmental impact in production
  - BUT: no perfect option exists
- The options explained:
  - Recyclable (paper, cardboard, some plastics)
  - Compostable (bagasse, PLA, bamboo)
  - Recycled content (rPET)
  - Biodegradable (vague term, be cautious)
- Reality check:
  - "Compostable" needs industrial facilities
  - "Recyclable" depends on local infrastructure
  - "Biodegradable" can mean anything
- Certifications to trust:
  - FSC (sustainable forestry)
  - OK Compost (verified compostable)
  - Seedling logo (European compost standard)
- Practical sustainable choices:
  - Kraft paper products (recyclable, renewable)
  - Bagasse (from waste sugarcane)
  - Bamboo (fast-growing, renewable)
  - rPET (recycled content)
